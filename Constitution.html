Dr. B.R. Ambedkar

The Architect of the Indian Constitution

Biography

Dr. Bhimrao Ramji Ambedkar, also known as Babasaheb, was an Indian jurist, economist, and social reformer who played a pivotal role in the drafting of the Indian Constitution. Born on April 14, 1891, in Mhow, Madhya Pradesh, Ambedkar was a staunch advocate for the rights of Dalits (formerly known as 'untouchables') and worked towards eradicating social inequality in India.

He was India's first law minister and served as the chairman of the drafting committee of the Constitution of India, ensuring that it enshrined the principles of justice, liberty, equality, and fraternity for all citizens, regardless of caste, creed, or religion.

Key Contributions

  • Architect of the Indian Constitution: Dr. Ambedkar played a leading role in the drafting of India's Constitution, ensuring it safeguarded the rights of the marginalized sections of society.
  • Social Reforms: He fought for the rights of Dalits and other oppressed classes, working towards the abolition of untouchability and caste-based discrimination.
  • Advocate for Women’s Rights: Dr. Ambedkar was an advocate for gender equality and worked to ensure women’s rights were addressed in the Indian Constitution.
  • Champion of Labor Rights: He worked towards the rights and welfare of laborers and workers, ensuring their rights were protected by law.
  • Educator: He was a scholar and an educator, earning degrees from prestigious institutions like Columbia University and the London School of Economics.
