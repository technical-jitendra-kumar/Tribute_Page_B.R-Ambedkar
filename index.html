"I measure the progress of a community by the degree of progress which women have achieved."

Biography

Dr. Bhimrao Ramji Ambedkar, popularly known as Babasaheb Ambedkar, was an Indian jurist, economist, social reformer, and the principal architect of the Indian Constitution. Born on April 14, 1891, he dedicated his life to eradicating social inequalities and uplifting marginalized communities.

  • 🎓 Education – Studied at Columbia University and the London School of Economics.
  • ⚖️ Constitution Maker – Played a key role in drafting the Indian Constitution.
  • 🗣 Social Reformer – Fought against caste discrimination and untouchability.
  • 📖 Author & Thinker – Wrote books like Annihilation of Caste & The Problem of the Rupee.
  • 🕉 Buddhist Revivalist – Led the mass conversion to Buddhism in 1956.

His legacy as a champion of equality, justice, and education continues to inspire millions. In recognition of his contributions, he was posthumously awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor.
